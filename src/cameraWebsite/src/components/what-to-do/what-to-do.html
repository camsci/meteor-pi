Is it a bird... is it a plane?

Once you've found a moving object, the next challenge is to work out what it is. MeteorPi cameras pick up large numbers of satellites and planes, as well as meteors.

Meteors move much more quickly than planes and satellites, at speeds of over 10 kilometres each second. They are usually only visible for a fraction of a second as they burn up.

Planes are often easy to spot because they have flashing lights. They are usually visible for 10-30 seconds, whereas meteors are gone in a fraction of a second.

Satellites, like planes, are visible for much longer than meteors – at least 10-30 seconds. They move at a steady speed and maintain a steady brightness.

The flight paths of satellites are extremely predictable, and so if you think you've found one, you can try looking on a website like In-The-Sky.org to see if you can track down which satellite it was.

Alternatively, why not look up which satellites flew over the UK last night, and see if any MeteorPi cameras picked them up?
